Discovering Psychology at Ashoka University

When I first stepped onto Ashoka University's campus in 2022, I was filled with a mixture of excitement and apprehension. Coming from Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalaya, I knew university life would be a significant transition. The sprawling campus with its modern buildings, diverse student body, and the promise of liberal education felt both intimidating and exhilarating.

My journey into psychology began with an introductory course that captivated my imagination. I remember sitting in that first lecture, listening to our professor explain how psychology bridges the gap between scientific methodology and understanding human experience. Something clicked for me in that moment – I realized psychology wasn't just about analyzing behavior; it was about understanding the complex tapestry of human existence.

The early semesters weren't without challenges. Adjusting to university-level coursework took time, and my initial GPA of 2.6 reflected that adjustment period. But what I love about Ashoka is the supportive academic environment. My professors noticed my passion for the subject despite my initial struggles and encouraged me to develop better study habits and engage more deeply with the material.

"The most valuable lessons often come from our greatest challenges. My academic journey at Ashoka taught me that persistence is just as important as intelligence."

Through consistent effort and developing a deeper connection with my coursework, I witnessed a remarkable transformation in my academic performance. By the Monsoon 2023 semester, I had earned a place on the Dean's List – an achievement I would maintain for three consecutive semesters as my GPA climbed to a perfect 4.0. This journey from struggle to success taught me more about resilience and growth mindset than any textbook could.

Hands-On Learning and Research

What makes psychology at Ashoka special is the emphasis on practical application alongside theoretical knowledge. In my Statistics and Data Research project, I had the opportunity to analyze real-world datasets using methods like ANOVA and T-tests. Working with tools like Jamovi and Excel, I discovered how statistical analysis could reveal meaningful patterns in human behavior.

One particularly memorable project involved analyzing consumer behavior patterns for a marketing study. We collected data from over 100 participants and used statistical methods to identify key factors influencing purchasing decisions. Through this project, I discovered how psychological principles could be applied to understand market trends and consumer preferences.

The research culture at Ashoka encourages students to explore interdisciplinary connections. My interest in marketing psychology led me to develop a challenger marketing strategy for lab-grown diamonds, targeting sustainability-conscious consumers. This project allowed me to combine psychological insights with business strategy, designing brand elements that would resonate with specific consumer values and motivations.

Similarly, my work on an integrated content strategy for Sleepy Owl taught me how psychological understanding of audience needs can inform effective marketing. By identifying targeted influencer partnerships and PR opportunities, I learned to translate psychological concepts into practical business applications.

Leadership and Community Engagement

My college experience extends far beyond the classroom. As a Campus Buddy at Ashoka University, I've had the privilege of guiding a visually impaired student through campus orientation. This experience has been profoundly rewarding, teaching me patience, empathy, and the importance of accessibility in education.

Working with visually impaired students has opened my eyes to the diverse needs within our campus community. I've organized inclusive events to foster community engagement, increasing participation of visually impaired peers by 30%. These events ranged from accessible game nights to discussion forums where students could share their experiences and educate others about accessibility needs.

One of my proudest achievements has been conducting digital literacy sessions for visually impaired students. Teaching them to navigate university platforms and use assistive technologies has empowered them to access resources independently. Seeing their confidence grow as they mastered these tools reinforced my belief in education as a path to empowerment.

Mentorship and Growth

In October 2024, I began my role as a Student Advisor with the Lodha Genius Program, mentoring five high school students from underprivileged backgrounds. These one-on-one sessions focus on holistic development, covering everything from academic planning to soft skills like communication and time management.

Mentoring these students has been a journey of mutual growth. While I guide them through their challenges, their perspectives and determination have inspired me to reflect on my own educational journey. There's something powerful about witnessing a student overcome a barrier they once thought insurmountable – whether it's mastering a difficult concept or finding the confidence to speak up in class.

My psychology background has proven invaluable in understanding the unique needs and motivations of each student. By applying concepts like growth mindset and personalized learning approaches, I've been able to tailor my mentorship to help each student find their own path to success.

Earlier in my college journey, I also served as a Teaching Assistant for the Lodha Genius Program, supporting a class of over 30 students through group discussions and personalized guidance. This experience taught me how to balance the needs of individual learners while maintaining group dynamics – a skill that continues to serve me in collaborative projects.

Looking Forward

As I continue my journey at Ashoka University, I'm excited to deepen my understanding of psychology through advanced coursework and research opportunities. I'm particularly interested in exploring the intersection of psychology, technology, and accessibility – inspired by my work with visually impaired students.

My goal is to develop research that could contribute to making education more accessible and inclusive for all learners. I believe psychology has a crucial role to play in identifying barriers to learning and developing evidence-based solutions to overcome them.

The community at Ashoka has shaped me in countless ways, teaching me that education is not just about acquiring knowledge but about developing the capacity to use that knowledge in service of others. As I look toward graduation in 2025, I'm grateful for the foundation Ashoka has provided and excited about the opportunities that lie ahead.
